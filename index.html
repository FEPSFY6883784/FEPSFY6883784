Abstract pre idv"Abstract_InText"> 

Early diagnosis of cancer is crucial for improving survival rates. Even though 80% of 
cancers are diagnosed at a late stage, recent advances in screening programmes and 
public health initiatives help improve the discover of cancer. One promising approach to 
early diagnosis is the use of blood-based liquid biopsies. However, assessing the 
effectiveness of early diagnosis requires a combination of process and outcome
measures. This article aims to discuss the progress made in early cancer detection,
monitoring the progress of early intervention, and improvement in early detection 
strategies.
